ARTICLE VI — NEGATIVE COVENANTS

Section 6.01. Incurrence of Indebtedness and Issuance of Preferred Stock.

The Borrower shall not, and shall not permit any of its Restricted Subsidiaries to, directly or
indirectly, create, incur, issue, assume, guarantee or otherwise become directly or indirectly
liable, contingently or otherwise (collectively, "incur"), with respect to any Indebtedness
(including Acquired Indebtedness); provided, however, that the Borrower and the Restricted
Subsidiaries may incur Indebtedness if, and only if, after giving pro forma effect to such
incurrence and the application of the net proceeds therefrom as if such incurrence had occurred
on the first day of the most recently ended four fiscal quarter period:

(a) the Total Net Leverage Ratio would be no greater than 3.50 to 1.00 (the "Leverage
Incurrence Test"); and

(b) the Interest Coverage Ratio would be no less than 3.00 to 1.00 (the "Coverage
Incurrence Test").

Both tests in clauses (a) and (b) must be satisfied concurrently. Satisfaction of one test alone
shall not permit incurrence.

Permitted Debt Baskets. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the following categories of Indebtedness
shall be permitted without satisfaction of the tests in clauses (a) and (b):

  (i)    Indebtedness under this Agreement and the other Loan Documents;

  (ii)   purchase money Indebtedness and Capital Lease Obligations incurred in the ordinary
         course of business, not to exceed $40,000,000 in aggregate outstanding principal
         amount at any time;

  (iii)  ordinary course trade payables and accrued liabilities arising in the normal course
         of business;

  (iv)   Indebtedness in respect of performance bonds, surety bonds, and letters of credit
         issued in the ordinary course of business, not to exceed $25,000,000 in aggregate
         face amount at any time outstanding;

  (v)    intercompany Indebtedness between the Borrower and any Wholly Owned Restricted
         Subsidiary or between Wholly Owned Restricted Subsidiaries, in each case subject
         to subordination provisions satisfactory to the Administrative Agent; and

  (vi)   a general basket not to exceed $50,000,000 in aggregate principal amount at any
         time outstanding (the "General Debt Basket"), which basket may not be used to
         incur Indebtedness that is secured by any Lien on Collateral.

For the avoidance of doubt, no grower component, builder basket, or ratio-based
expansion shall apply to the General Debt Basket in clause (vi) above. The baskets in
clauses (ii) through (vi) are not cumulative with one another to the extent they would
otherwise overlap.

EBITDA Calculations. For purposes of calculating Consolidated EBITDA under this Section 6.01,
no add-backs, adjustments, or pro forma effect shall be given to projected cost savings,
synergies, or restructuring charges unless such items (A) have been realized or are
contractually committed to be realized within six months following the relevant transaction,
(B) are reasonably identifiable and factually supportable based on third-party evidence,
and (C) in aggregate do not exceed 10% of Consolidated EBITDA calculated without any
add-backs for the same period. No run-rate adjustments or "agreed EBITDA" provisions shall
apply to this Agreement.

Section 6.04. Restricted Payments.

The Borrower shall not, and shall not permit any Restricted Subsidiary to, directly or
indirectly, declare, order, pay, make, or set apart any sum for any Restricted Payment, unless,
at the time of and after giving pro forma effect to such Restricted Payment:

(a) no Default or Event of Default shall have occurred and be continuing or would result
    therefrom;

(b) the Total Net Leverage Ratio on a pro forma basis shall be no greater than 3.00 to 1.00
    (the "RP Leverage Test"); and

(c) the aggregate amount of all Restricted Payments made in any fiscal year pursuant to
    this clause shall not exceed $35,000,000 (the "Annual RP Cap").

The Annual RP Cap is an absolute fixed ceiling and does not contain any grower component,
basket builder, or Available Amount mechanic. Unused Annual RP Cap amounts from any
prior fiscal year may not be carried forward to a subsequent fiscal year.

Permitted Restricted Payments. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the following Restricted
Payments shall be permitted without regard to clause (b) or (c) above, provided that no
Event of Default has occurred and is continuing:

  (i)    dividends or distributions by any Restricted Subsidiary to the Borrower or to
         any other Wholly Owned Restricted Subsidiary;

  (ii)   repurchases of Equity Interests of the Borrower from employees, officers, or
         directors upon termination of employment or service, not to exceed $10,000,000
         in aggregate per fiscal year;

  (iii)  payments to satisfy tax withholding obligations in connection with the settlement
         of equity awards, not to exceed $5,000,000 per fiscal year.

For the further avoidance of doubt, no Restricted Payment may be made by virtue of any
portability provision, ratio carve-out, or deemed compliance mechanism. Each Restricted
Payment must independently satisfy the conditions in clauses (a) through (c) above at the
time of payment.

Section 6.05. Asset Sales.

The Borrower shall not, and shall not permit any Restricted Subsidiary to, make any Asset
Sale unless each of the following conditions is satisfied:

(a) the Borrower or the applicable Restricted Subsidiary receives consideration at the
    time of such Asset Sale at least equal to the Fair Market Value (as determined in good
    faith by the board of directors of the Borrower and certified to the Administrative
    Agent) of the assets or Equity Interests sold or otherwise disposed of;

(b) not less than 85% of the total consideration received in respect of such Asset Sale
    shall consist of cash or Cash Equivalents, received at closing; and

(c) the Net Proceeds from such Asset Sale shall, within 180 days of receipt:

    (i)   first, be applied to prepay outstanding Term Loans on a mandatory basis at par,
          without premium or penalty, on a pro rata basis across all tranches; or

    (ii)  second, to the extent the Borrower elects in writing delivered to the
          Administrative Agent within 30 days of receipt, be reinvested in assets used
          or useful in the Borrower's then-existing lines of business; provided that
          such reinvestment shall be completed within 180 days of the date of receipt
          of Net Proceeds (with no extension permitted); and

    (iii) any Net Proceeds not applied pursuant to clauses (i) or (ii) above within 180
          days shall be applied immediately as a mandatory prepayment of Term Loans.

Permitted Asset Sales. The following dispositions shall be permitted without restriction:

  (A) dispositions of obsolete, worn out, or surplus equipment or inventory in the
      ordinary course of business;

  (B) sales of goods and services in the ordinary course of business consistent with
      past practice;

  (C) any single transaction or series of related transactions with aggregate Fair
      Market Value not exceeding $15,000,000 per transaction and $30,000,000 in
      aggregate across all such transactions in any fiscal year; and

  (D) any involuntary disposition resulting from a casualty event or condemnation,
      subject to the reinvestment and prepayment mechanics in clause (c) above.

For the avoidance of doubt, no permitted Asset Sale basket shall apply to the sale or
other disposition of any Collateral that constitutes real property, registered Intellectual
Property, or Equity Interests in any Material Subsidiary, regardless of the value thereof,
without the prior written consent of Required Lenders. Sale-leaseback transactions,
synthetic lease arrangements, and receivables securitizations are not permitted under
this Section 6.05 or otherwise under this Agreement.

Section 6.09. Collateral and Guarantees.

(a) Grant of Security Interest. The Borrower shall, and shall cause each Material Domestic
    Subsidiary and each Restricted Subsidiary that guarantees any other material
    Indebtedness of the Borrower to, grant to the Collateral Agent for the benefit of
    the Secured Parties a first priority, perfected Lien (subject only to Permitted Liens)
    on all of the following assets, whether now owned or hereafter acquired:

    (i)    all accounts receivable, contract rights, and payment intangibles;
    (ii)   all inventory (including raw materials, work in process, and finished goods);
    (iii)  all equipment, machinery, and fixtures;
    (iv)   all owned and leasehold real property (regardless of value);
    (v)    all Intellectual Property registered or applied for in the United States, the
           European Union, or any jurisdiction representing more than 5% of consolidated
           revenues;
    (vi)   all Equity Interests in each direct and indirect Restricted Subsidiary
           (limited to 65% of voting Equity Interests in any first-tier CFC);
    (vii)  all cash, deposit accounts, securities accounts, and investment property; and
    (viii) all proceeds and products of the foregoing.

(b) Collateral Coverage Ratio. The Borrower shall maintain, as of the last day of each
    fiscal quarter, a Collateral Coverage Ratio (defined as the ratio of (x) the
    aggregate appraised Fair Market Value of all Collateral as most recently determined
    by an independent third-party appraisal conducted within the preceding 12 months,
    to (y) the aggregate outstanding principal amount of all Secured Obligations) of
    not less than 2.00 to 1.00. If the Collateral Coverage Ratio falls below 2.00 to 1.00
    at any measurement date, the Borrower shall, within 30 days, either (A) prepay
    Secured Obligations in an amount sufficient to restore compliance or (B) grant
    additional Collateral satisfactory to the Collateral Agent in its reasonable
    discretion.

(c) Guarantors. Each Material Domestic Subsidiary (defined as any Restricted Subsidiary
    with total assets or total revenues exceeding $10,000,000) shall become a Guarantor
    and shall execute and deliver to the Collateral Agent a Guarantee Agreement, Security
    Agreement, and all related perfection documents within 30 days of becoming a Material
    Domestic Subsidiary. The Guarantee shall be unconditional, irrevocable, and shall cover
    all Secured Obligations on a joint and several basis. No Guarantor may be released from
    its Guarantee except upon (i) a bona fide arm's-length sale of such Guarantor to an
    unaffiliated third party that is permitted under Section 6.05, with 100% of Net Proceeds
    applied to mandatory prepayment of Term Loans, or (ii) the prior written consent of
    Required Lenders obtained pursuant to Section 9.02(b).

(d) After-Acquired Property. The Borrower shall, and shall cause each Guarantor to,
    promptly (and in any event within 15 Business Days) after the acquisition of any
    property or asset with a Fair Market Value in excess of $1,000,000, grant to the
    Collateral Agent a first priority perfected Lien on such property or asset, and deliver
    to the Administrative Agent all related certificates, instruments, and filings necessary
    to perfect such Lien.

(e) Appraisals. The Collateral Agent shall have the right to require, at the Borrower's
    expense, an independent third-party appraisal of all or any portion of the Collateral
    at any time during the continuance of a Default or Event of Default, and no more than
    once per fiscal year during all other periods.

(f) No Negative Pledge Carve-Out. No asset of the Borrower or any Restricted Subsidiary
    that constitutes Collateral shall be subject to any Lien other than Permitted Liens,
    regardless of whether such Lien would otherwise constitute a Permitted Lien under a
    different provision of this Agreement.

Section 9.02. Amendments, Waivers, and Consents.

(a) Supermajority Lender Consent. No amendment, modification, supplement, restatement,
    or waiver of any provision of this Agreement or any other Loan Document shall be
    effective unless approved in writing by Supermajority Lenders (meaning Lenders holding
    more than 66⅔% of the aggregate outstanding principal amount of all Loans and unused
    Revolving Commitments at the time of such vote) (the "Supermajority Lender Consent").

(b) Unanimous Lender Consent. Notwithstanding clause (a), the following amendments shall
    require the written consent of each Lender directly or indirectly affected thereby,
    and no such amendment shall be effective without such unanimous consent:

    (i)    any reduction in the principal amount, or forgiveness of any portion, of any
           Loan or Reimbursement Obligation;
    (ii)   any reduction in the rate of interest payable on any Loan (other than a waiver
           of default interest applicable uniformly to all Lenders);
    (iii)  any extension of the scheduled maturity or final maturity date of any Loan,
           or any extension of any scheduled amortization payment;
    (iv)   any increase in the Revolving Commitment of any Lender;
    (v)    any release of all or substantially all of the Collateral (with "substantially
           all" defined as Collateral with an aggregate Fair Market Value exceeding 10%
           of total Collateral value);
    (vi)   any release of any Guarantor that is a Material Domestic Subsidiary, except
           as expressly permitted by Section 6.09(c);
    (vii)  any amendment or waiver of this Section 9.02 or the definition of "Required
           Lenders," "Supermajority Lenders," or "Event of Default"; and
    (viii) any subordination of the Liens of the Collateral Agent on all or any material
           portion of the Collateral to the Liens of any other Person.

(c) No Snooze/Extend. This Agreement does not contain any provision permitting the
    Borrower to extend the maturity of any Loan held by a consenting Lender without
    the consent of all affected Lenders as required by clause (b)(iii) above. Any
    purported extension of maturity of any Loan without such consent shall be null and
    void and of no legal force or effect.

(d) No Portability. No change of control, merger, consolidation, or sale of all or
    substantially all assets of the Borrower shall be deemed to constitute compliance
    with any covenant or condition of this Agreement by any successor entity. Any
    successor or surviving entity shall independently satisfy all conditions and
    covenants in this Agreement at the time of such transaction.

(e) Non-Consenting Lender Replacement. If any Lender does not consent to an amendment
    requiring only Supermajority Lender Consent under clause (a), the Borrower may
    replace such non-consenting Lender with an Eligible Assignee, provided that (i)
    the replacement Lender assumes all obligations of the replaced Lender at par plus
    accrued interest, (ii) no Default or Event of Default has occurred and is continuing,
    and (iii) the Borrower has obtained the prior written consent of the Administrative
    Agent (not to be unreasonably withheld).

(f) Anti-Yank-the-Bank Restriction. No amendment may disproportionately reduce the
    economic rights or voting rights of any individual Lender relative to the other
    Lenders without the prior written consent of such affected Lender.

ARTICLE VII — FINANCIAL MAINTENANCE COVENANTS

Section 7.01. Total Net Leverage Ratio.

The Borrower shall not permit the Total Net Leverage Ratio as of the last day of any
fiscal quarter (commencing with the fiscal quarter ending March 31, 2025) to exceed:

  Fiscal Quarters Ending                   Maximum Ratio
  March 31, 2025 — December 31, 2025      3.50 to 1.00
  March 31, 2026 — December 31, 2026      3.25 to 1.00
  March 31, 2027 and thereafter            3.00 to 1.00

Section 7.02. Interest Coverage Ratio.

The Borrower shall not permit the Interest Coverage Ratio as of the last day of any
fiscal quarter to be less than:

  Fiscal Quarters Ending                   Minimum Ratio
  March 31, 2025 — December 31, 2025      3.00 to 1.00
  March 31, 2026 and thereafter            3.50 to 1.00

Section 7.03. Minimum Liquidity.

The Borrower shall maintain, at all times, unrestricted cash and Cash Equivalents plus
undrawn availability under the Revolving Credit Facility of not less than $50,000,000.

Section 7.04. No Equity Cure.

This Agreement does not contain any equity cure right, equity cure mechanic, or any
provision permitting the Borrower to remedy a failure to comply with Sections 7.01 or 7.02
by making an equity contribution or otherwise injecting capital. Any failure to comply with
Sections 7.01 or 7.02 shall constitute an immediate Event of Default under Article VIII.

ARTICLE VIII — EVENTS OF DEFAULT

Section 8.01. Events of Default.

Each of the following shall constitute an Event of Default:

(a) failure to pay any principal of any Loan when due (whether at stated maturity, upon
    acceleration, or otherwise), and such failure is not cured within 1 Business Day;

(b) failure to pay any interest on any Loan or any fee or other amount payable hereunder
    within 5 Business Days after the due date thereof;

(c) any representation or warranty made by any Loan Party in any Loan Document shall
    have been incorrect in any material respect when made, and, if curable, not cured
    within 20 days of notice thereof;

(d) failure to comply with any covenant in Article VI or Article VII, provided that with
    respect to Article VI (other than Sections 6.01, 6.04, and 6.05), such failure is
    not cured within 30 days of notice; failure to comply with Sections 6.01, 6.04, 6.05,
    or Article VII shall constitute an immediate Event of Default without any cure period;

(e) a Change of Control shall occur (defined as any Person or group acquiring beneficial
    ownership of more than 35% of the voting Equity Interests of the Borrower, or the
    board of directors ceasing to consist of a majority of Continuing Directors);

(f) any Loan Document shall cease to be in full force and effect, or any Lien granted
    under any Security Document shall cease to be a perfected first priority Lien on any
    material portion of the Collateral (subject to Permitted Liens), and such failure is
    not cured within 5 Business Days.
